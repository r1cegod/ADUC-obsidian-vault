> **TL;DR**: Band 8+ annotated process diagram report. Recycling plastic bottles — 8 stages.

## PROMPT

> The diagram below illustrates the process by which used plastic bottles are recycled into new products. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

**DATA (imagined diagram — 8 stages):**
1. Collection from kerbside bins
2. Transport to sorting facility → separated by plastic type
3. Washing — labels and contaminants removed
4. Shredding into flakes
5. Melting
6. Filtering — impurities extracted
7. Pelletizing — cooled into uniform pellets
8. Manufacture of new plastic products

---

## RESPONSE

The diagram illustrates the eight-stage process by which used plastic bottles are recycled and transformed into new products.

Overall, the process begins with the collection of used bottles and involves a sequence of mechanical and thermal stages before the material is ultimately converted into reusable pellets for manufacturing. The entire cycle can be broadly divided into a preparation phase and a transformation phase.

In the first stage, used plastic bottles are collected from households via kerbside recycling bins. They are then transported to a dedicated sorting facility, where different types of plastic are separated from one another. Following this, the sorted bottles are thoroughly washed to remove labels and any remaining contaminants.

Once cleaned, the plastic is mechanically shredded into small flakes, which are subsequently melted at high temperatures. The molten material is then passed through a filtering system, where residual impurities are extracted to ensure purity. In the penultimate stage, the filtered plastic is cooled and formed into uniform pellets. Finally, these pellets serve as raw material for the manufacture of new plastic products, completing the recycling cycle.
