> **TL;DR**: Band 8+ annotated pie chart report. Electricity production by source, 2000 vs 2020 (two charts).

## PROMPT

> The two pie charts below illustrate the proportion of electricity generated from five sources in a country in 2000 and 2020. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

**DATA (imagined charts):**

| Source      | 2000 | 2020 |
|-------------|------|------|
| Coal        | 50%  | 20%  |
| Oil         | 25%  | 18%  |
| Gas         | 15%  | 22%  |
| Nuclear     | 8%   | 15%  |
| Renewables  | 2%   | 25%  |

---

## RESPONSE

The two pie charts illustrate changes in the proportion of electricity generated from five different sources in a country between 2000 and 2020.

Overall, the most striking development over this period was the dramatic decline in coal's dominance and the remarkable rise of renewable energy. By 2020, electricity production had become considerably more diversified compared with 2000.

In 2000, coal accounted for the largest share at 50%, nearly double the proportion contributed by oil at 25%. Gas and nuclear represented 15% and 8% of output respectively, while renewables comprised a negligible 2%.

By 2020, the picture had shifted substantially. Coal's share had more than halved, falling to 20%, and renewables had surged to become the single largest source at 25% — an increase of 23 percentage points over two decades. Gas rose modestly to 22%, narrowly surpassing coal as the second-largest contributor. Nuclear's share nearly doubled from 8% to 15%, while oil saw only a marginal decline to 18%.

These changes collectively indicate a significant transition away from fossil fuels towards cleaner energy sources, though coal and oil together still accounted for 38% of production by 2020.

## BAND 8+ MOVES

```
"remarkable rise" / "dramatic decline"  ← evaluative adjectives matched to scale of change
Overview: no data, names the two        ← both key changes stated cleanly
 biggest shifts
"comprised a negligible 2%"             ← precise collocation; "negligible" = Band 8 word
"had more than halved"                  ← active analytical language, not passive listing
"narrowly surpassing coal"              ← precise comparison; Band 5 just lists both values
Final sentence: caveat paragraph        ← Band 8 move — qualifying the trend shows full analysis
```
