> **TL;DR**: Band 8+ annotated table report. Tourist arrivals, revenue, and revenue per visitor for 5 countries, 2018.

## PROMPT

> The table below shows data on tourist arrivals, total revenue, and revenue per tourist for five countries in 2018. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

**DATA:**

| Country | Arrivals (millions) | Revenue (billion USD) | Revenue/tourist (USD) |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------------|----------------------|
| France  | 89.4               | 67.4                  | 754                  |
| USA     | 79.6               | 214.5                 | 2,695                |
| Spain   | 82.7               | 73.8                  | 893                  |
| China   | 62.9               | 38.8                  | 617                  |
| UK      | 37.7               | 52.3                  | 1,388                |

---

## RESPONSE

The table presents data on tourist arrivals, total revenue generated, and average revenue per visitor for five countries in 2018.

Overall, France attracted the greatest number of tourists, but the USA generated by far the highest total revenue. Revenue per visitor varied enormously across the five nations, with American tourists spending considerably more on average than those visiting the other countries.

France led in arrivals with 89.4 million visitors, closely followed by Spain at 82.7 million and the USA at 79.6 million. China and the UK recorded notably fewer tourists at 62.9 and 37.7 million respectively.

In terms of revenue, however, the ranking shifted dramatically. The USA generated $214.5 billion, more than three times France's figure of $67.4 billion, despite receiving fewer tourists. Spain and the UK earned $73.8 billion and $52.3 billion respectively, while China recorded the lowest revenue at $38.8 billion.

The most striking disparity appeared in revenue per tourist. American visitors spent an average of $2,695 each — more than double the UK figure of $1,388 and over four times the $617 recorded for China. France and Spain both fell below $900 per visitor despite their high overall arrival numbers.

## BAND 8+ MOVES

```
Overview: two different rankings      ← most arrivals ≠ most revenue; this IS the insight
"the ranking shifted dramatically"    ← signals analytical move; Band 5 just lists both
"despite receiving fewer tourists"    ← causal/contrastive link showing understanding
"most striking disparity"             ← evaluative framing; selects what matters
"over four times the $617"            ← ratio comparison; Band 8 characteristic
Final ¶: per-tourist reading          ← adds a third angle; shows full analytical range
```
