> **TL;DR**: Band 8+ annotated adv-dis essay (balanced, no position). Topic: working from home.

## PROMPT

> More and more people are choosing to work from home rather than commuting to an office. What are the advantages and disadvantages of this development?

---

## BRAINSTORM (before writing)

```
Body 1 (advantages): Claim — autonomy + efficiency gains
                     Mechanism — no commute time lost, workers control environment
                     Example — Stanford economist Nicholas Bloom study: 13% productivity gain
Body 2 (disadvantages): Claim — social isolation + collaboration barriers
                          Mechanism — spontaneous interaction lost, junior staff miss mentorship
                          Example — Apple/Amazon partial reversals citing innovation concerns
```

---

## RESPONSE

Remote work has become a defining feature of contemporary employment, accelerated by the widespread disruption of the COVID-19 pandemic. There are compelling arguments on both sides of this development, and neither the benefits nor the drawbacks should be overlooked.

The primary advantage of working from home is the significant increase in personal autonomy and daily efficiency it affords workers. Without the time and cost of daily commuting, employees can redirect those hours towards productive work or personal wellbeing. Research by Stanford economist Nicholas Bloom found that remote workers demonstrated productivity gains of approximately 13% compared with office-based counterparts. Additionally, employers benefit from reduced overhead costs as the need for large office spaces diminishes, allowing resources to be directed elsewhere. These gains are real and measurable across a wide range of industries.

On the other hand, remote work introduces notable social and organisational disadvantages. The absence of physical co-presence makes spontaneous collaboration difficult, and many workers report growing feelings of isolation during extended periods of home working. Junior employees are particularly affected, as they miss out on informal mentorship and the observational learning that occurs naturally in shared workspaces. Several major companies, including Apple and Amazon, have partially reversed remote working policies citing concerns about innovation and team cohesion — suggesting that some costs of distributed work are not easily offset by individual productivity gains.

In conclusion, the rise of home working offers clear benefits in terms of flexibility and efficiency, but introduces meaningful challenges around collaboration and social connection. A hybrid model — preserving the advantages of both arrangements — is likely to represent the most practical and durable solution for most organisations.

## BAND 8+ MOVES

```
Thesis: no position stated             ← "compelling arguments on both sides" = balanced type signal
"affords workers"                      ← formal register; not "gives workers" or "lets workers have"
"These gains are real and measurable"  ← closes ¶1 with confidence; Band 8 paragraph control
"particularly affected"                ← identifies a specific subgroup; analytical precision
"not easily offset by"                 ← economic register; shows understanding of trade-offs
Conclusion: hybrid model proposed      ← Band 8 move — balanced type CAN offer nuanced middle ground
```
