> **TL;DR**: Band 8+ annotated bar chart report. Weekly leisure hours by activity and age group, 2018.

## PROMPT

> The bar chart below shows the average number of hours per week that three age groups spent on four leisure activities in 2018. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

**DATA (imagined chart):**

| Activity   | 18–30 | 31–50 | 51+  |
|------------|-------|-------|------|
| Internet   | 8.5   | 6.1   | 2.4  |
| Television | 3.8   | 5.0   | 7.2  |
| Sport      | 5.2   | 3.1   | 1.8  |
| Reading    | 1.5   | 2.5   | 4.2  |

---

## RESPONSE

The bar chart compares the average number of hours per week that three age groups devoted to four leisure activities in 2018.

Overall, internet use was highest among the youngest group and declined sharply with age, while television viewing and reading showed the opposite pattern. Sport participation decreased progressively across all three groups.

Among 18–30 year olds, internet use dominated at 8.5 hours per week, followed by sport at 5.2 hours. Television and reading accounted for a comparatively modest 3.8 and 1.5 hours respectively.

For those aged 31–50, the pattern shifted considerably. Internet use fell to 6.1 hours, while television rose to 5 hours, narrowing the gap between the two activities to just over one hour. Sport declined to 3.1 hours and reading remained relatively low at 2.5 hours.

The 51+ group showed the most pronounced differences. Television became the dominant activity at 7.2 hours, while internet use dropped dramatically to just 2.4 hours — a reduction of more than 6 hours compared with the youngest cohort. Reading climbed to 4.2 hours, and sport fell to its lowest point of 1.8 hours across all three groups.

## BAND 8+ MOVES

```
"devoted to"                        ← precise collocation; not "used for" or "did"
Overview: opposing trends named     ← internet down with age, TV up — clear analysis
"narrowing the gap to just over"    ← analytical phrase, not just listing numbers
"most pronounced differences"       ← signals deliberate grouping strategy
"a reduction of more than 6 hours   ← compares across groups; Band 5 just states values
 compared with"
"dominant activity"                 ← evaluative language, Band 8 characteristic
```
