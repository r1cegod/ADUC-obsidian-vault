> **TL;DR**: Band 8+ annotated line graph report. Internet usage (%) in 3 countries, 2000–2020.

## PROMPT

> The line graph below shows the percentage of the population using the internet in three countries between 2000 and 2020. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

**DATA (imagined chart):**
- Country X: 8% → 85% (steady rise, slight plateau after 2015)
- Country Y: 20% → 92% (fastest early growth, highest throughout)
- Country Z: 3% → 55% (consistently lowest, slow but steady)

---

## RESPONSE

The line graph illustrates changes in internet usage rates across three countries over a twenty-year period from 2000 to 2020.

Overall, all three nations experienced substantial growth in internet adoption, with Country Y maintaining the highest penetration rate throughout the period. Country X followed a broadly similar trajectory, while Country Z, despite consistent growth, lagged considerably behind the other two.

In 2000, Country Y led with 20% of its population online, compared with 8% in Country X and just 3% in Country Z. Over the following fifteen years, Country Y expanded rapidly, climbing to approximately 85% before levelling off and reaching a peak of 92% by 2020. Country X mirrored this upward trend, surging from 8% to around 80% by 2015, after which growth plateaued at 85%.

Country Z, by contrast, grew at a markedly slower pace throughout the entire period, rising steadily from 3% to approximately 55% by 2020. While this represents a significant increase in absolute terms, the gap between Country Z and the other two nations widened over time, with a difference of over 30 percentage points separating it from Country Y by the period's end.

## BAND 8+ MOVES

```
"illustrates changes in"          ← precise verb for trend data, not just "shows"
Overview: trend named + leader     ← no data in ¶2, just features — Task Achievement
"levelling off" / "plateaued"     ← Band 8 vocabulary range, not just "stopped rising"
"mirrored this upward trend"      ← collocation; Band 5 would say "also increased"
"by contrast" + "markedly slower" ← natural cohesion, not mechanical "however"
"widened over time"               ← comparative language showing analysis, not just description
```
