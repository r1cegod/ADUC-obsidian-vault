> **TL;DR**: Ben AI treats Obsidian as a broad AI operating system whose root instruction file routes agents through stable context, daily continuity, projects, reusable resources, skills, tasks, and team knowledge.

## Summary
This video pushes the vault concept toward a business or personal OS. Instead of focusing only on source ingest, it emphasizes persistent context: who you are, what your projects are, what reusable resources exist, and how agents should navigate all of it through one root instruction file. The value of the source is structural breadth, not precision. It is useful for deciding which kinds of context deserve their own folders, but it is much looser than the Karpathy or Cole sources on source-of-truth discipline and compilation quality.

## Key Points
- A root instruction file is the navigation contract for the whole vault.
- Stable context deserves its own dedicated layer, not repeated explanation in every chat.
- Daily continuity is one of the highest-value parts of the system.
- Resources, skills, tasks, and team files make the vault reusable across workflows.
- Obsidian's value is visualization, navigation, linking, and shared context.

## Details
### What the source teaches well
It is strong at folder-role thinking. If the vault is going to support many workflows, not just article ingest, then it needs places for stable context, active projects, reusable assets, and daily continuity.

### Limits
The source under-specifies quality control. It does not deeply answer how agent-written material should be separated from human-written notes, how to lint for contradictions, or how to keep context inflation from turning into clutter.
