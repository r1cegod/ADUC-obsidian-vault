> **TL;DR**: Discord recommends treating staff channels as a deliberate workspace: clear informational channels at the top, social and coordination spaces in the middle, restricted leadership channels below, and privacy-aware logging throughout.

## Summary
This article turns “make a mod channel” into a fuller staff-operations architecture. Discord recommends private moderation categories with informational channels, team chat, update or memo channels, training and leadership channels, and any restricted subspaces needed for specialized work. One of the most important points is privacy: staff spaces are not automatically fair game for universal logging or universal staff access. That makes this source especially valuable for evidence handling and confidentiality boundaries.

## Key Points
- Staff categories should be structured around real workflows, not just hidden copies of public channels
- Internal rules, updates, and training material should be easy for all moderators to find
- Logging should respect access boundaries and avoid overexposing restricted discussions

## Details
The article's best contribution is its warning against careless transparency. If only part of the team can access a leadership or sensitive case channel, deleted or edited content from that space should not necessarily be pumped into a broader log. That is a direct operational constraint for moderation privacy. It also reinforces that moderation teams need a social space, not just enforcement spaces, because trust and rapport reduce coordination failure.
